NORTON & STOCKTON ANCIENTS LADIES FC

YORK CITY, AWAY, WON 6-1

NERWFL Premier League
York City Ladies vs Norton and Stockton Ancients Ladies

Norton travelled to York knowing that the game would be difficult; York being the only team to take points from Norton. The visitors made two changes from last week's home win over Scunthorpe, with Jess White taking the place of injured Becca Davison and Nyci Thorns coming in for Kate McSorley who played 90 minutes for the club's under 18's side yesterday. 
York kicked off and tried to pin Norton back by putting balls into the corner and boxing the away side in, however it wasn't long before Norton were into their stride and creating chances. Norton opened the scoring when a Jess White cross was met by the head of Nyci Thorns rushing in at the back post and powering it past the 'keeper with only 11 minutes on the clock. Norton doubled their lead only 3 minutes later when a through ball from Anna Mawston found Sian Williams who smashed the ball into the bottom corner. Norton were now well on top and the midfield three of White, Hutchinson and McGovern were dominating play in the middle and the three forwards continued to cause York's defence all kinds of problems. It was only a matter of time before Norton would extend their lead and that came on the stroke of half time when a ball over the top from White saw Mawston bravely challenge the home team's 'keeper in a 50-50 and head the ball over her into the empty net. Norton came in at half time 3-0 up without York testing 'keeper Chisnall due to a fine defensive performance from Stabler, Johnson, Beetch and Rodger.

Half Time 
York City 0-3 Norton

Norton started the second half where they left off in the first half, putting York under a lot of pressure due to some high pressing and intense work rate. Norton added a fourth when substitute Kate McSorley played Anna Mawston through with her first touch of the game and she cooly chipped the ball over the 'keeper. The fifth goal came on 58 minutes when Nyci Thorns' shot was deflected into the goal by a York defender. Credit to York who continued to battle and tried to play football the right way, and as a result they were rewarded when a ball into the box saw Norton appeal for offside, however it wasn't given and the York player placed the ball past Chisnall for a consolation goal for the hosts. Norton quickly replied when Jess White's corner went over everyone and found its way into the back of the net making it 6-1. Norton could have added further goals but a crossbar denied Nyci Thorns and some poor finishing kept the score at 6. The last 20 minutes were quiet for both teams; Norton knowing that the job was done and York who had given their all in the first 70 minutes. The final whistle went, with Norton now needing 7 points for the title. Overall it was a great team performance and the effort and work rate from every player was superb. Next up is a little break from league action as Norton welcome York City Reserves to Station Road for the league cup quarter-final next week.

Full Time
York City Ladies 1-6 Norton Ladies

Player of the Match
