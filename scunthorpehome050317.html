NORTON & STOCKTON ANCIENTS LADIES FC

SCUNTHOPRE & BOTTESFORD (H), WON 2-0



NERWFL Premier League

Norton and Stockton Ancients Ladies vs Scunthorpe Ladies
Having played well against Tynedale the previous week, the ladies were looking to continue their run against a Scunthorpe team that lost in a close encounter against top of the league Barnsley in their last game. The ladies made one change from last week’s team with Becca Davison returning from injury, coming in for Jess White who was unavailable, resulting in Kate McSorley moving into the front three.
Norton started quickly and put Scunthorpe under pressure straight away and it looked like it was only a matter of time before the home team would break the deadlock. Norton did take the lead in the ninth minute when a ball over the top from Heather Johnson found Sian Williams, who rounded the keeper and tapped the ball into the empty net. Norton quickly doubled their lead when a pass from midfield found the feet of McSorley who turned and a played a short pass to Williams who hit a first time shot past the Scunthorpe keeper. At this point Norton were looking like they were going to get more goals, however Scunthorpe upped their work rate and looked to stop Norton from playing. Norton nearly scored a third when Aimee Stabler's shot from outside the area was saved by the keeper. Norton became sloppy in possession and were giving the ball away too many times in good areas, however Scunthorpe rarely caused Norton's back line of Rodger, Beetch, Johnson and Stabler any problems, with goalkeeper Chisnall touching the ball only from back passes from her teammates.

Half Time
Norton Ladies 2 - 0 Scunthorpe Ladies

The second half was a strange one as Scunthorpe continued to work hard and frustrate Norton but the home team seemed to be playing in second gear. Chances were few and far between but Norton never looked like conceding a goal throughout the game. The game was all being played in the Scunthorpe half and Norton probably had more corners in this game than they have had in the last 3 games added together but failed to capitalise on their territorial advantage and with the game coming to a close Mawston broke through but her left foot shot went wide of the post. Mawston and Hutchinson both picked up yellow cards in the dying minutes of the game, showing the frustration that the team felt both towards their own performances and that of the opposition. The full time whistle blew and Norton, for the first time this season, moved to top of the league. Overall it was far from Norton’s best performance of the season and but at this stage of the season it's the result that matters and the run continues.

Full Time
Norton Ladies 2 - 0 Scunthorpe Ladies

Player of the Match
Sian Williams
