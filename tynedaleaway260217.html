NORTON & STOCKTON ANCIENTS LADIES FC

TYNEDALE LADIES FC, AWAY, WON 4-1

NERWFL Premier League
RACA Tynedale Ladies vs Norton and Stockton Ancients Ladies
Norton were away for the second week running to a Tynedale side who currently find themselves in the quarter-final of the League Cup and the final of the County Cup so today's game was always going to be a tough encounter. The weather conditions were far from perfect with a wind blowing from goal to goal and the pitch was a little uneven too.
Norton made one change from the side the started last week; with Kate McSorley coming in for the injured Becca Davison. 
Norton won the toss and chose to kick against a strong wind, but started really well and on the first attack a McSorley through ball found Williams and her shot was saved by the 'keeper. Norton took an early foothold in the game, playing well and moving the ball around quickly, when after only 6 minutes, a long pass from Vicki Hutchinson sent Sian Williams clear and she finished in style. Norton quickly doubled their lead when a McSorley pass found Williams and she rifled the ball into the top corner to make it 2-0. Tynedale then began to get on top and were unlucky not to pull one back when their centre forward broke clear and rounded the 'keeper only to see her shot come back off the post. Norton's back four of Stabler, Beetch, Johnson and Rodger had their work cut out with Tynedale's strong and physical forwards but coped well with the pressure that was put on them. With the half drawing to an end, the home side's pressure eventually paid off when a ball over the top found the striker who chipped the 'keeper to make it 2-1 at the break.

Half Time
Tynedale Ladies 1-2 Norton Ladies

The second half saw the wind behind Norton which allowed players to get 10 yards higher up the pitch. The first chance came Norton's way when a Hutchinson free kick was spilled by the Tynedale 'keeper and Mawston was unable to find the net with the rebound. Norton began to get on top with the midfield three of Hutchinson, McGovern and McSorley all winning personal battles and driving Norton forward. The 3rd goal eventually came when Tynedale's goalkeeper threw the ball out to a midfielder before Hutchinson robbed her of possession and lofted the ball back over the 'keeper's head from 30 yards. Tynedale then had a chance to get one back when a corner was played into the Norton box and a well struck shot cannoned back off the crossbar. The game was eventually sealed when Kate McSorley found Jess White on the edge of the area and her left foot shot found the bottom corner for her second goal in as many games. Norton saw out the remaining minutes to come away with a massive 3 points against good opposition in difficult conditions.
Full Time

RACA Tynedale Ladies 1-4 Norton and Stockton Ancients Ladies

Goal Scorers
