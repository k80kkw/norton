MATCH REPORTS

RESERVES

Durham County League Premier Division
Birtley Town Ladies v Norton & Stockton Ancients Ladies Reserves


Norton made the trip up to Birtley for the season opener full of confidence after a good friendly performance in midweek. Norton started with the wind at their backs and quickly pushed Birtley back. Norton began to create chances and Hamilton had two chances to score but her first was saved well by the keeper and her second rolled agonisingly wide. Norton's next chance came to Tierney when she burst on to a Glen through-ball but shot wide. Sophie Tierney then turned provider knocking two great balls through, but Hamilton shot wide and Munro had her shot saved for a corner. From the resulting corner the ball fell to Jones and her shot was blocked. Then out of nothing Birtley played a long ball forward and the Birtley striker latched on to it and gave the home side a shock 1-0 lead.
Norton almost equalised immediately when Munro broke clear and blasted the ball straight at the 'keeper only for the rebound to fall back to her feet, but she shot over. Norton did eventually equalise when Hamilton put a great ball across goal and Munro was on hand to open her league account. Norton then took complete control of the midfield with Tierney and Etherington playing some excellent football from the middle. Chances fell to Atkinson and Tierney but both players had their shots blocked by some excellent defending from the Birtley back line. Norton did take the lead 10 minutes before half time when a Charlie-Jo Glen cross was met on the volley at the back post by Louise Atkinson. Mia Brannigan then came close to adding a third when her effort from outside the box went narrowly wide.

Half Time


Birtley Town Ladies 1 - 2 Norton & Stockton Ancients Ladies Reserves
Norton started the second half strong and scored almost immediately when Laura Etherington played a through-ball that Frayah Munro was able to take in her stride and finish superbly. Minutes later Norton added a fourth; this time Etherington sent Hamilton away and she placed the ball under the on-rushing 'keeper. Frayah Munro then broke clear and looked like she was going to score her third but the Birtley defender brought her down just outside the box. Not to be denied of her hat-trick however, shortly after, Munro latched on to an Emily Joynes pass and finished calmly to make it 5-1.
Norton's added a sixth when some good play from Tierney found Glen who cut in from the left and rifled a shot in off the bar with her right foot. Norton had a number of chances to increase the lead but none were taken until Paige Hunter cut in from the right wing and hit a shot that was destined for goal, only to be denied by the hand of a defender. The resulting spot kick was placed into the top corner by Caitlin Jones making it 7-1. Norton had plenty of chances to increase the lead but none were taken and anything Birtley tried to throw at Norton was dealt with by Nat Wild and Emily Joynes, who were excellent throughout the game. Another special mention to Lauryn Morgans who didn't have the busiest of days but was very vocal and excellent with her feet when playing out from the back.

Final Score
Birtley Town Ladies 1 - 7 Norton & Stockton Ancients Reserves
